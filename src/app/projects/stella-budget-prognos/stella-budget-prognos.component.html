Stakeholder Request → Reframed Problem

The initiative originated from headquarters stakeholders — mainly business controllers and managers — who requested dashboards to provide quick budget overviews and support faster action. Early discussions revealed the root issue was not visualization alone. Users struggled with navigation, information structure, and finding key actions across complex budget types. This reframed the assignment from “add dashboards” to “understand the workflow and problems across roles.”

This is a typical table in the budget application today - before any design updates. I've replaced the real data with dummy data.

Organizational Dynamics

Several departments expressed concern that previous initiatives were HQ-centric and did not represent users working closer to operational execution. This became a critical factor in how research was designed and how participants were selected.

Exploratory Interviews and User Tests

To ensure representation, I partnered with an experienced business controller from an underrepresented department to co-create an interview and user test script focused on workflows, pain points, tool switching, and data dependencies. Because recordings were prohibited, I reqruited a UX designer from another department took detailed notes during sessions.

Scope

  • 10 users
  • 60-minute individual sessions
  • 3 user roles: business controllers, operations coordinators, managers
  • 3 departments represented across different mission areas

Analysis Through Affinity Mapping

The insights were continuously validated with a business controller to ensure domain accuracy and reduce interpretation bias.

Affinity map of the tagged data.

Key Findings

  • Challenges vary greatly depending on departmental budgets due to varying task complexity:
    • Personnel / maintenance budgets
    • Volume budgets linked to welfare program delivery
  • Reliance on external tools
    • Excel for most budget work (forcing manual synchronization)
    • PowerPoint for visualization instead of built-in capabilities
    • Calculators for converting values between reports

These bottlenecks affected both productivity and data reliability.

Problem Statements

Based on findings, I developed concise, actionable problem statements that reframed needs from “feature requests” into “workflow and information architecture needs” to guide upcoming design phases.

Next Steps

I will translate problem statements into validated design updates in production. Planned approach:

  • Structured ideation with users to explore solutions
  • Technical feasibility vs problem relevance matrix with developers to prioritize improvements
  • Iterative validation through prototypes, user tests and stakeholder alignment
