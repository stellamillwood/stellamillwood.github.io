Stakeholder Need

The stakeholder group that initiated the project consisted mainly of business controllers and managers at Swedish Public Employment Office's headquarters. Originally, they requested that I create dashboards on the start page of the budget application, which would allow for easy access overviews of the budget status, making it easier to take action if need be. However, in dialogue with the group it became more clear that navigation and information structure were, according to them, problem areas. At this point it was clear to me that we needed a better understanding of the problem.

Exploratory Interviews and User Tests

There was a worry in some departments that the user group would not be adequately represented, as some felt that earlier initiatives had been skewed toward a headquarter-centric perspective. Due to that, and due to the applications requirement of omderate to high level expertise, I collaborated with a business controller (high expertise) to create an interview and user test guide that that oriented at identifying painpoints. The business controller worked at a department that felt they had been underrepresented in earlier intitatives. That department's work is central to the government agency's core mission. There were multiple colleagues at headquarter's who confirmed a tendency to skew earlier initatives to an HQ-centric perspective. We also identified 10 relevant users from across the organization who would represent the user group. A UX designer from a different department than mine took notes because recordings were prohibited, and we collected user data during two weeks.

Affinity map of the results.

I expected task complexity to vary greatly between roles, but the analysis also made clear that the complexity varied greatly between departments due to different types of budgets (personel and general maintenance budget versus volume budget of welfare programs). This made me reflect on what requirements I had on myself to understand the work. I set up soft boundaries for myself tha tI could adhere to in order to understand user problems without deep diving into the data.

The results also showed that there was quite a lot of manual data handling going on - creating bottlenecks. Examples include users using calculators to calculate values between reports in the system, importing the data into Excel and doing most work there - putting pressure on keeping data synchronized between Excel and the budget application, and the use of Powerpoint for data visualization rather than the budget application's built in visualization capabilities.

Based on the findings I created problem statements aiming at being concise and actionable.

Next Steps

In the coming three months I will work on transforming these problem statements into user tested design updates in production. My general plan is to conduct a structured brainstorming with users, and then narrow down the ideas using a matrix for technical feasbility versus problem relevance together with developers. I want to strike a balance between responding to the users needs in terms of the tasks they perfomr in the application for which they lack proper support today, and responding to the stakeholder request of simplifying the overviews by means of dashboards. Through quick iterations with users I want to move from sketches to a stakeholder accepted high fidelity prototype that can be put into production.
