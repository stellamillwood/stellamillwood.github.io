This was a two-day project where I pitched in to design the UI of EMPLOYERS new AI-based postal system. Every day the government agency receives thousands of documents from citizens, ranging from medical documents to documents proving right to unemployment funds (A-kassa). As I had previosuly worked as temporary product owner on the old postal system, I had knowledge of the users, their tasks and what the new system was trying to solve.

The Users and Their Tasks

Users are administrators at EMPLOYER and handle incoming documents sent through post by citizens daily. They handle the documents by identifying the social security number (personnummer), identifying what type of document it is and importing it manually to the correct system accordingly to be handled by the next administrator. The AI technology speeds up this process by identifying the infromation more efficiently. The new system creates a queue of document where a user can assign or unassign themselves documents, rather than choose a file in a file manager as it previously worked. It also allows the user to send the document to the correct system through the click of a button, rather than importing it manually. The development team did not consist of any UX-designers, but they had a sketch in Figma, as seen below.

My contributions

The Queue Page and Working Space Page

I redesigned the UI using EMPLOYERS design system and applied our design patterns. For the queue (arbetskö) and work space (arbetsyta) view I focused on language clarity and adding explanations when needed. I focused on making the developers' conceptual model visible to users. For example, in the original sketch the system said "Put back 117 document in the queue" followed by a button "Put back" and I changed that to "You have 43 unhandled documents" followed by a button for "Put back documents in queue". The developer team were suggesting adding a "User Support" page, but I chose to add preambles to communicate the purpose of the different pages. I think this was important because the developer teams' model for what the interaction step by step should look like was quite innovative, but also new to the users. I also made their UI elements more space efficient. The excess space could either be left as is for a more clean look, or they can add content if need in the future - which is more likely. The Document Page

In the view for actually handling the document data I worked with the hierarchy and using different level headings and sections to categorize form fields. I also added heading when needed. For example the original sketch had input field "Month" and "Year", but no heading to explain what the month and date were in reference to. I added "Aktivitetsrapporten gäller för" to make it clear to users that the month and date were in reference to the period during which the citizen was applying for unemployment funds. This was particularly important considering there were multiple field for filling out date. I also changed the button text at the bottom from "Delete" to "Download and delete" to make it clear to the users what would happened if they pressed the button. I also added field and form validation - informing users whether any information was incorrect or missing. If the users pressed "Register document" I also added information at the bottom of the page to show users that the document was successfully registered and that the user was now looking at a new document, and could view the old document using the ID (which was part of the text).

In Retrospect...

I would've been cautious to make the Queue page and the Working space page two separate pages. I think the page where you see the document could be considered the Working Space rendering the Working Space page unneccessary, but this is something that could be a topic for user testing. In general I also think the copy has room for improvement. I would have wanted to make the button "Download and delete" even more lear to users since the idea behind it may be a bit unclear. I also wasn't a fan of showing the message "The document was registered successfully. You are now looking at a new document" since I felt that wasn't the most clear way to communicate that it was a new document the users was looking at (they all look very similar).
